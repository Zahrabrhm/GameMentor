How GameMentor Helps

The Mario AI uses a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) trained via Deep Q-Learning on raw pixel inputs. It learns to navigate levels by processing visual information similar to how humans see the game.

GameMentor identifies critical moments - like jumps over pits or enemy encounters - where your decision differed significantly from the optimal action.

How GameMentor Works

1

Train Expert AI

We train a Deep Reinforcement Learning agent to master the game. For Lunar Lander, we use Double DQN. For Super Mario, we use CNN-based DQN.

2

Record Human Play

While you play, we record your actions and the game states. Simultaneously, we query the AI for what it would do at each state.

3

Identify Mistakes

We compare human actions to AI recommendations. States with high Q-value differences are "critical" - where mistakes matter most.

4

Generate Tutorial

The tutorial replays your game up to the mistake, then shows the AI demonstrating the correct approach from that point.

5

Practice & Improve

Practice mode recreates the exact scenario where you struggled, letting you retry until you master it.

Technical Details

Lunar Lander Agent

  • Algorithm: Double Deep Q-Network
  • State: 8-dimensional vector
  • Actions: 4 discrete
  • Network: 3-layer MLP (128-64-4)

Super Mario Agent

  • Algorithm: DQN with CNN
  • State: 84x84x4 grayscale frames
  • Actions: 7 discrete
  • Network: 3 Conv + 2 Dense layers

Mistake Detection

  • Q-value difference threshold
  • Critical state identification
  • Action comparison analysis
